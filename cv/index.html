Work

  • 2025.01 - present

    Raleigh, NC

    Research Scientist - Power System Optimization
    Hitachi Energy Research
    • Developed a four-wire, three-phase unbalanced load flow (UBLF) engine to serve as a core analytical component for ADMS software.
    • Formulated mathematical models for a wide range of network components along with the underlying mismatch equations and Jacobian matrix, establishing foundation for high-fidelity network analysis.
    • Validated and benchmarked the engine's performance against existing UBLF solver, OpenDSS, and established IEEE test feeder results.
  • 2024.05 - 2024.08

    Richmond, VA

    Graduate Intern, Electric Transmission Strategic Initiatives
    Dominion Energy
    • Created a PSS/E model of the Coastal Virginia Offshore Wind (CVOW) system and conducted a transfer limit study using TARA to determine the maximum power transfer capacity from CVOW before requiring system upgrades.
    • Performed generation deliverability studies (with PJM rules) under different dispatch scenarios to identify potential thermal violations and executed phasor-domain transient stability studies to analyze system stability and frequency response under sudden loss of CVOW units.
  • 2024.01 - 2024.05

    Atlanta, GA

    Graduate Intern, Advanced System Analytics & Modeling
    North American Electric Reliability Corporation (NERC)
    • Developed Python scripts to automate the assessment of steady-state power flow and transient dynamics Case Quality Metrics, improving sustainability, efficiency, and accuracy in evaluating model performance in PSLF software.
    • Analyzed disturbances in BESS across the Western Interconnection using data from GOs, TOs, and WECC cases.
  • 2023.05 - 2023.08

    Richmond, VA

    Graduate Intern, Electric Transmission Planning-Modeling
    Dominion Energy
    • Developed and validated transmission planning models to support long-term transmission planning for data centers in northern Virginia.
